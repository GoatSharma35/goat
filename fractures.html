Fracture First Aid

Stabilize and seek immediate medical care

üö® Call 911 Immediately If:

  • Bone is protruding through skin
  • Person is unconscious or unresponsive
  • Heavy bleeding
  • Suspected neck or back injury
  • Limb appears deformed or bent unnaturally

Immediate Care Steps:

  1. Don't move the person - Unless they're in immediate danger
  2. Check for breathing and pulse - Start CPR if needed
  3. Control bleeding - Apply pressure with clean cloth around (not on) bone
  4. Immobilize the injury - Support above and below the fracture site
  5. Make a splint - Use rigid materials like boards, magazines, or rolled newspapers
  6. Apply ice - Wrap in cloth, apply to area to reduce swelling
  7. Treat for shock - Keep person warm and calm

‚ùå Don't Do This:

  • Don't try to realign the bone
  • Don't push protruding bones back in
  • Don't give food or water
  • Don't move the person unless absolutely necessary

Signs of a Fracture:

  • Severe pain
  • Swelling and bruising
  • Deformity or abnormal angle
  • Inability to use the limb normally
  • Grinding sensation when moving
