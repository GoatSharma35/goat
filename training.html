üö® Basic Steps When Approaching an Emergency Scene

1. Scene Safety Assessment (FIRST PRIORITY)

  • Stop and Look: Survey the scene before approaching
  • Check for Hazards: Fire, electrical wires, traffic, violence, unstable structures
  • Ensure Personal Safety: Never become a victim yourself
  • Use PPE: Gloves, eye protection if available
  • Call for Help: Dial 911 immediately if scene is dangerous

2. Initial Assessment

  • Number of Victims: Count how many people need help
  • Mechanism of Injury: What happened? (fall, car accident, etc.)
  • Obvious Life Threats: Severe bleeding, not breathing, unconscious
  • Bystander Information: Ask witnesses what they saw

3. Primary Survey (ABC)

  • A - Airway: Is the airway clear and open?
  • B - Breathing: Is the person breathing normally?
  • C - Circulation: Check pulse, look for severe bleeding
  • D - Disability: Check for spinal injury (don't move unless necessary)
  • E - Exposure: Look for other injuries, maintain body temperature

4. Call for Emergency Services

  • Call 911: If not already done
  • Provide Clear Information: Location, number of victims, type of emergency
  • Follow Dispatcher Instructions: They may guide you through care
  • Don't Hang Up: Stay on line unless told otherwise

5. Provide Care

  • Treat Life-Threatening Issues First: Severe bleeding, breathing problems
  • Keep Victim Calm: Reassure and explain what you're doing
  • Monitor Constantly: Watch for changes in condition
  • Document: Remember what you did for emergency responders

  • Treat all blood and body fluids as potentially infectious
  • Wear gloves when possible
  • Avoid direct contact with blood/fluids
  • Wash hands thoroughly after giving care
  • Use barriers (cloth, plastic) if no gloves available

Comprehensive Injury Information

Detailed information about different types of injuries and their treatment.

ü©∏ Cuts & Wounds

Types: Abrasions (scrapes), lacerations (cuts), punctures, avulsions (torn skin)

Severity Signs: Deep cuts, arterial bleeding (bright red, spurting), numbness, inability to move

Treatment Priority: Control bleeding first, then clean and dress wound

When to Call 911: Severe bleeding, deep cuts, cuts on neck/chest, signs of shock

üî• Burns

Types: First-degree (red skin), Second-degree (blisters), Third-degree (white/charred skin)

Causes: Heat, chemicals, electricity, radiation, friction

Rule of 9s: Head=9%, each arm=9%, chest=18%, back=18%, each leg=18%, genitals=1%

Critical Burns: >10% body area, face/hands/feet/genitals, airway involvement

ü¶¥ Fractures

Types: Closed (skin intact), Open (bone visible), Greenstick (partial), Comminuted (shattered)

Signs: Deformity, swelling, pain, inability to use, grinding sensation

Complications: Blood vessel damage, nerve damage, infection (open fractures)

Immobilization: Splint above and below injury, check circulation frequently

ü´Å Breathing Emergencies

Signs of Distress: Rapid breathing, use of accessory muscles, tripod position, cyanosis

Asthma: Wheezing, tight chest, difficulty speaking, use of rescue inhaler

Anaphylaxis: Rapid onset, whole-body reaction, requires epinephrine

Pneumothorax: Sudden chest pain, shortness of breath, unequal chest movement

üíä Poisoning

Routes: Ingestion (swallowed), Inhalation (breathed), Absorption (skin), Injection

Common Poisons: Medications, household chemicals, carbon monoxide, plants, alcohol

Antidotes: Activated charcoal (some ingestions), specific antidotes for certain poisons

Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 (available 24/7)

üå°Ô∏è Temperature Emergencies

Heat Exhaustion: Heavy sweating, weakness, nausea, normal mental status

Heat Stroke: High temperature, altered mental status, hot/dry skin, life-threatening

Hypothermia: Body temperature <95¬∞F, shivering, confusion, slurred speech

Frostbite: Frozen tissue, white/gray skin, hard/waxy feel, numbness

‚ù§Ô∏è Cardiac Emergencies

Heart Attack: Chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, sweating, arm pain

Cardiac Arrest: No pulse, not breathing, unconscious - requires immediate CPR

AED Use: Turn on, follow prompts, ensure no one touching during shock

CPR Ratios: 30 compressions : 2 breaths, push hard and fast 100-120/min
