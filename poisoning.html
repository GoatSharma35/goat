Poisoning Emergency

Quick action saves lives

üö® Call Poison Control IMMEDIATELY:

Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

Or call 911 for severe symptoms

Signs of Poisoning:

  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal pain
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Confusion or drowsiness
  • Seizures
  • Burns around mouth (chemical poisoning)
  • Strange odor on breath

Immediate Steps:

  1. Assess the situation - Is the area safe?
  2. Check breathing and consciousness - Start CPR if needed
  3. Call Poison Control - 1-800-222-1222
  4. Identify the poison - Save container/packaging
  5. Follow Poison Control instructions - They will guide specific treatment
  6. Monitor vital signs - Stay with person until help arrives

For Swallowed Poisons:

  • DO NOT induce vomiting - Unless instructed by Poison Control
  • Remove any remaining substance - From mouth with finger
  • Give water or milk - Only if person is conscious and can swallow
  • Keep person calm - Sit upright if conscious

For Skin Contact:

  1. Remove contaminated clothing - Avoid spreading poison
  2. Flush with water - For 15-20 minutes
  3. Remove jewelry - Before swelling occurs
  4. Don't use ointments - Unless instructed

For Inhaled Poisons:

  1. Get to fresh air immediately - Away from contaminated area
  2. Loosen tight clothing - Around neck and chest
  3. Check breathing - Start rescue breathing if needed
  4. Don't enter contaminated area - Without proper protection

‚ùå Never Do This:

  • Don't induce vomiting with corrosive substances
  • Don't give activated charcoal unless instructed
  • Don't give food or drink to unconscious person
  • Don't assume small amounts are harmless
  • Don't delay calling for help

Common Household Poisons:

  • Medications - Overdose of prescription or OTC drugs
  • Cleaning products - Bleach, ammonia, toilet bowl cleaners
  • Plants - Some houseplants and wild plants
  • Cosmetics - Nail polish, perfume, makeup
  • Automotive products - Antifreeze, windshield washer fluid

Prevention Tips:

  • Store chemicals in original containers
  • Keep medicines and chemicals locked away from children
  • Never mix cleaning products
  • Read labels before using any product
  • Install carbon monoxide detectors
  • Keep Poison Control number handy: 1-800-222-1222
