Choking Emergency

Act fast - every second counts

Signs of Choking:

  • Cannot speak, cough, or breathe
  • Makes high-pitched sounds when trying to breathe
  • Hands clutching at throat
  • Face turns red, then blue
  • Loss of consciousness

For Adults & Children Over 1 Year:

  1. Ask "Are you choking?" - If they can't speak, act immediately
  2. Call 911 - Or have someone else call
  3. Give 5 back blows - Lean person forward, hit firmly between shoulder blades
  4. Give 5 abdominal thrusts - Stand behind person, place hands above navel, thrust inward and upward
  5. Continue alternating - 5 back blows, 5 abdominal thrusts until object comes out
  6. If person becomes unconscious - Start CPR

For Infants Under 1 Year:

  1. Support head and neck - Hold baby face-down on your forearm
  2. Give 5 back blows - Use heel of hand between shoulder blades
  3. Turn baby over - Support head, turn face-up on your forearm
  4. Give 5 chest thrusts - Use 2 fingers on center of chest, just below nipples
  5. Continue alternating - Until object comes out or baby becomes unconscious
