CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

Life-saving emergency procedure

üö® When to Use CPR:

  • Person is unconscious and not breathing normally
  • No pulse can be detected
  • Person is not responsive to verbal or physical stimuli

CPR Steps for Adults:

  1. Call 911 immediately - Or have someone else call
  2. Position the person - On back on firm, flat surface
  3. Tilt head back - Lift chin to open airway
  4. Place hands correctly - Heel of one hand on center of chest between nipples, other hand on top
  5. Push hard and fast - Compress at least 2 inches deep at 100-120 beats per minute
  6. Let chest recoil - Allow complete chest recoil between compressions
  7. Give 30 compressions - Then 2 rescue breaths
  8. Continue cycles - 30 compressions, 2 breaths until help arrives

Rescue Breathing:

  1. Tilt head back - Lift chin
  2. Pinch nose closed - Create seal over mouth
  3. Give 2 breaths - Each breath 1 second, watch chest rise
  4. Return to compressions - Don't delay

Hands-Only CPR:

If you're not trained in rescue breathing, continuous chest compressions are still highly effective:

  • Place hands on center of chest
  • Push hard and fast, at least 2 inches deep
  • Don't stop compressions until help arrives
  • Push to rhythm of "Stayin' Alive" (100-120 BPM)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Notes:

  • Don't be afraid to push hard - broken ribs heal, cardiac arrest is fatal
  • Switch with another person every 2 minutes to prevent fatigue
  • Continue until emergency services arrive or person starts breathing
  • Get proper CPR training from certified instructors
  • Consider learning to use an AED (Automated External Defibrillator)

For Children (1-8 years):

  • Use one or two hands for compressions
  • Compress about 2 inches or 1/3 chest depth
  • Give gentler rescue breaths
  • Same ratio: 30 compressions to 2 breaths

For Infants (under 1 year):

  • Use 2 fingers for compressions
  • Compress about 1.5 inches
  • Cover mouth AND nose for rescue breaths
  • Same ratio: 30 compressions to 2 breaths

CPR - Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation

Life-saving emergency procedure

üö® When to Perform CPR:

  • Person is unconscious and not breathing normally
  • No pulse (if you're trained to check)
  • Person is unresponsive to verbal and physical stimuli
  • Cardiac arrest suspected

CPR Steps (Adults):

  1. Call 911 immediately - Or have someone else call
  2. Position the person - On their back on a firm surface
  3. Tilt head back - Lift chin to open airway
  4. Place hands correctly - Heel of one hand on center of chest, other hand on top
  5. Push hard and fast - At least 2 inches deep, 100-120 compressions per minute
  6. Give rescue breaths - 2 breaths after every 30 compressions
  7. Continue until help arrives - Don't stop CPR

Hand Position for Chest Compressions:

  • Place heel of one hand on the breastbone
  • Position between the nipples
  • Place other hand on top, interlocking fingers
  • Keep arms straight, shoulders directly over hands
  • Use your whole body weight, not just arms

Rescue Breathing:

  1. Tilt head back - Lift chin to open airway
  2. Pinch nose closed - Use thumb and index finger
  3. Make seal over mouth - Cover with your mouth
  4. Give 2 breaths - Each breath should last 1 second
  5. Watch for chest to rise - With each breath

CPR for Children (1-8 years):

  • Use one or two hands for compressions
  • Compress at least 1/3 the depth of chest (about 2 inches)
  • Same compression rate: 100-120 per minute
  • 30 compressions to 2 breaths ratio

CPR for Infants (under 1 year):

  • Use two fingers for compressions
  • Place fingers on breastbone, just below nipples
  • Compress at least 1/3 the depth of chest (about 1.5 inches)
  • Cover infant's mouth and nose with your mouth for rescue breaths

‚ùå Don't Do This:

  • Don't be afraid to push hard - broken ribs heal, but brain damage doesn't
  • Don't stop compressions for more than 10 seconds
  • Don't perform CPR on a conscious person
  • Don't tilt head back if spinal injury suspected

Remember: Push Hard, Push Fast, Don't Stop

Even imperfect CPR is better than no CPR. The most important thing is to start compressions immediately and continue until professional help arrives.
