Breathing Problems & Asthma Attacks

Emergency respiratory care

üö® Signs of Severe Breathing Problems:

  • Difficulty breathing - Shortness of breath, wheezing
  • Chest tightness - Feeling like chest is being squeezed
  • Rapid breathing - More than 20 breaths per minute
  • Blue lips or fingernails - Sign of oxygen deprivation
  • Cannot speak in full sentences - Due to breathlessness
  • Anxiety or panic - Feeling of impending doom

Immediate Treatment for Asthma Attack:

  1. Help person sit upright - Leaning slightly forward
  2. Stay calm - Reassure the person
  3. Find their inhaler - Usually a blue "rescue" inhaler
  4. Help use inhaler - 4-10 puffs through spacer if available
  5. Wait 5-10 minutes - Check if breathing improves
  6. Call 911 if no improvement - Or symptoms worsen
  7. Continue rescue medication - As directed while waiting for help

How to Use an Inhaler Properly:

  1. Remove cap and shake - Shake inhaler 5-10 times
  2. Breathe out fully - Empty lungs completely
  3. Seal lips around mouthpiece - Make tight seal
  4. Press and breathe in slowly - Deep, steady breath
  5. Hold breath for 10 seconds - Or as long as comfortable
  6. Breathe out slowly - Wait 30-60 seconds between puffs

üö® Call 911 Immediately If:

  • Person cannot speak due to breathlessness
  • Lips or fingernails turn blue
  • Person becomes drowsy or confused
  • Inhaler provides no relief after 15 minutes
  • Breathing becomes increasingly difficult
  • Person loses consciousness

For Other Breathing Difficulties:

  • Panic attack - Help person breathe slowly, use paper bag if hyperventilating
  • Allergic reaction - Use antihistamine, watch for anaphylaxis signs
  • Choking - Perform back blows and abdominal thrusts
  • Heart attack - Call 911, give aspirin if not allergic

‚ùå Don't Do This:

  • Don't leave person alone
  • Don't give food or drink
  • Don't make person lie down
  • Don't use someone else's inhaler without permission
  • Don't assume it will pass on its own

Prevention Tips:

  • Know your asthma triggers and avoid them
  • Always carry rescue inhaler
  • Take controller medications as prescribed
  • Have an asthma action plan
  • Regular check-ups with doctor
