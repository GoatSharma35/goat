Heat Stroke & Cold Exposure

Temperature-related emergencies

üî• HEAT STROKE - MEDICAL EMERGENCY

üö® Signs of Heat Stroke (Call 911):

  • High body temperature - Above 104¬∞F (40¬∞C)
  • Hot, dry skin - Or profuse sweating
  • Altered mental state - Confusion, agitation, slurred speech
  • Rapid, strong pulse - Then becomes weak
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Seizures
  • Loss of consciousness

Heat Stroke Treatment:

  1. Call 911 immediately - This is life-threatening
  2. Move to cool area - Indoors or shade
  3. Remove excess clothing - Help body cool down
  4. Cool the body rapidly - Ice packs on neck, armpits, groin
  5. Fan the person - While applying cool water to skin
  6. Monitor breathing - Be ready to perform CPR
  7. Don't give fluids - If person is unconscious or vomiting

Heat Exhaustion (Less Severe):

  • Move to cool place - Rest in air conditioning or shade
  • Remove tight clothing
  • Apply cool, wet cloths - To skin
  • Give cool water - If conscious and able to drink
  • Seek medical attention - If no improvement in 30 minutes

üßä COLD EXPOSURE EMERGENCIES

üö® Signs of Hypothermia:

  • Shivering - Uncontrollable at first, then stops
  • Confusion - Slurred speech, poor coordination
  • Drowsiness - Difficulty staying awake
  • Weak pulse - Slow, shallow breathing
  • Loss of consciousness

Hypothermia Treatment:

  1. Call 911 - For severe hypothermia
  2. Move indoors - Get out of cold environment
  3. Remove wet clothing - Gently, avoid rough handling
  4. Warm gradually - Blankets, warm (not hot) drinks
  5. Cover head and neck - Heat escapes quickly from these areas
  6. Monitor breathing - Be prepared for CPR
  7. Don't use direct heat - No heating pads or hot water

Frostbite Treatment:

  1. Move to warm area - Get out of cold
  2. Remove wet items - Clothing, jewelry
  3. Soak in warm water - 104-108¬∞F for 15-30 minutes
  4. Take pain medication - Rewarming can be painful
  5. Protect the area - Loosely bandage with gauze
  6. Don't walk on frostbitten feet - Unless absolutely necessary
  7. Seek medical attention - For severe frostbite

‚ùå Don't Do This:

  • Heat stroke: Don't give aspirin or acetaminophen
  • Heat stroke: Don't give fluids if unconscious
  • Hypothermia: Don't use direct heat sources
  • Hypothermia: Don't massage frostbitten areas
  • Frostbite: Don't thaw if refreezing is possible

Prevention:

  • Hot weather: Stay hydrated, wear light clothing, avoid peak sun hours
  • Cold weather: Layer clothing, keep dry, cover exposed skin
  • Know the warning signs - Act early before emergency develops
  • Check on vulnerable people - Elderly, young children, those with medical conditions
